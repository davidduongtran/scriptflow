Privacy Policy — ScriptFlow

Last updated: May 2026

ScriptFlow is a Chrome extension for AI-assisted YouTube script and video prompt generation. This policy explains what data is involved when you use the extension.

Data We Do NOT Collect

We do not operate any servers or backend infrastructure. We do not collect, receive, store, or process any of your data on our end. There are no analytics, telemetry, or tracking of any kind.

Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The extension stores the following data in Chrome's local storage, on your device only:

  • Your Anthropic (Claude) API key
  • Your Google Gemini API key
  • Analyzer data received from companion TITANSYS extensions
  • Generated scripts and VEO prompts (history)
  • Your settings and preferences (template, model, style choices)

This data never leaves your device except as described in the sections below. You can delete all locally stored data at any time via Settings → Clear All Local Data.

Data Sent to Third-Party AI Providers

When you generate scripts or video prompts, your input content is sent directly from your browser to the following services using your own API keys:

  • Anthropic (api.anthropic.com) — for script generation via the Claude API. See Anthropic's Privacy Policy.
  • Google (generativelanguage.googleapis.com) — for VEO prompt generation via the Gemini API. See Google's Privacy Policy.

These requests are made directly from your browser. We are not a party to these transmissions and have no access to the content you send or the responses you receive.

Data Retention

All data stored by the extension remains on your device until you:

  • Clear it manually via Settings → Clear All Local Data
  • Uninstall the extension
  • Clear Chrome's local storage for the extension

Children's Privacy

This extension is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy occasionally. Continued use of the extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision.
